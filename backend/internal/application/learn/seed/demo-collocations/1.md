---THEORY---
## A — Words that go together
Some words are used together so often that they sound natural as a pair. For example, we **make a decision** but we **do the housework**. Choosing the right partner word is a big part of sounding fluent.

| We say … | We don't say … |
| --- | --- |
| make a mistake | do a mistake |
| do your homework | make your homework |

## B — Common partners
- **make**: make a plan, make a promise, make progress, make breakfast
- **do**: do the shopping, do exercise, do the dishes, do homework

Learning these pairs together is easier than learning the words on their own.

## C — Why it matters
Using the natural partner makes your English sound clear and confident — even with simple words.
---EXERCISES---
## 1.1 -> A
Complete with make or do.
1. I need to {{make}} a decision about the trip.
2. Did you {{do}} the shopping yesterday?
3. She wants to {{make}} progress in English.
4. Please {{do}} the dishes after dinner.
5. He always {{does}} his homework before dinner.
6. Can we {{make}} a plan for the weekend?

## 1.2 -> B
Sort the phrases into the correct group.
~~~sort
make: a plan, a promise, a mistake, breakfast
do: the dishes, exercise, the shopping, homework
~~~

## 1.3 -> A, B
Choose the natural partner.
1. {{*make|do}} a promise
2. {{*do|make}} exercise
3. {{*make|do}} breakfast
4. {{*do|make}} the shopping

## 1.4 -> A
Find the word partners in the text.
~~~highlight
Every morning I ==make a plan== for the day and try to ==do some exercise==. Then I ==make breakfast== and ==do the dishes== before I leave for work. At school I ==do my homework== carefully so I don't ==make a mistake==.
~~~
