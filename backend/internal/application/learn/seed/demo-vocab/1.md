## 1 — gather
**gather** *(verb)* - to bring things or people together into one place.
~~~image
A clear educational illustration of "gather": a person collecting scattered apples into a basket.
~~~
- We gather in the kitchen every morning to plan the day.
- She gathered her notes before the meeting started.
- A small crowd gathered to watch the street musician.

## 2 — wander
**wander** *(verb)* - to walk slowly with no fixed direction.
~~~image
A clear educational illustration of "wander": someone strolling along the winding paths of a quiet park.
~~~
- We spent the afternoon wandering through the old town.
- The dog likes to wander around the garden.
- My thoughts wander whenever the room is too quiet.

## 3 — mend
**mend** *(verb)* - to repair something that is broken or torn.
~~~image
A clear educational illustration of "mend": hands sewing a patch onto a torn jacket.
~~~
- Can you mend this shirt before winter?
- He mended the fence after the storm.
- A cup of tea can mend almost any bad morning.

## 4 — glance
**glance** *(noun/verb)* - a quick short look.
~~~image
A clear educational illustration of "glance": a person taking a fast look at a clock on the wall.
~~~
- She gave the letter a quick glance and put it away.
- He glanced at his phone during the meeting.
- One glance told me something was wrong.

## 5 — sturdy
**sturdy** *(adjective)* - strong and not easily broken.
~~~image
A clear educational illustration of "sturdy": a solid wooden table standing firmly on four thick legs.
~~~
- These boots are sturdy enough for the mountains.
- We built a sturdy shelf out of old planks.
- The bridge looked old but was surprisingly sturdy.

## 6 — spare
**spare** *(verb/adjective)* - to give time or money you can afford; extra.
~~~image
A clear educational illustration of "spare": a person handing over a spare key to a neighbour.
~~~
- Can you spare a few minutes to help me?
- We keep a spare tyre in the car.
- She spends her spare time painting.

## Words in Use
Read the short passage and notice how the new words work together.

### A Quiet Saturday
On Saturday mornings I **wander** through the market, then **gather** a few friends for coffee. We keep a **spare** chair at the table for anyone who drops by. Last week Tom asked me to **mend** his bag strap — one quick **glance** told me it was still **sturdy** enough to fix.
